# Seattle Local News & Information Sources

Staying informed about Seattle and its surrounding areas is easier with a curated list of reliable local news outlets and community sources. Below, we've categorized **50+ high-quality, free** sources – including major news publications, neighborhood blogs, tech/business outlets, and social media feeds – that provide daily or weekly updates on Seattle’s news, events, and culture. Each source is active, frequently updated, and accessible without a paid subscription.

## Major Seattle News Publications

- **The Seattle Times** – The city’s major daily newspaper, offering comprehensive coverage of local news, business, sports, and more. The Seattle Times is an independent, Pulitzer-winning paper and the most widely read in the Pacific Northwest. (Website: seattletimes.com)
- **Seattle Post-Intelligencer (Seattle PI)** – A historic Seattle newspaper now published exclusively online. Seattle PI provides unfettered local news, weather, traffic, real estate, and sports coverage on its website. (Website: seattlepi.com)
- **The Stranger** – Seattle’s iconic alternative weekly (tagline: “Seattle’s Only Newspaper”), known for its edgy coverage of local news, politics, arts, music, and culture. The Stranger’s print edition is free, and its website offers daily blogs and event listings.
- **Seattle Weekly** – Once a prominent print weekly, now an online-only publication focusing on Seattle news, culture, food, and entertainment. Seattle Weekly connects readers to the city through reported stories and insightful interviews. (Website: seattleweekly.com)
- **Real Change** – A weekly street newspaper sold by vendors, devoted to reporting on homelessness, social justice, and underrepresented voices. Real Change provides a voice for low-income and homeless communities in Seattle. (Website: realchangenews.org)

## Local TV and Radio News Outlets

- **KING 5 News (NBC Seattle)** – A top-rated local TV news station. KING 5 delivers breaking news, weather updates, traffic, and community stories across broadcast and digital channels. (Website: king5.com)
- **KOMO News (ABC Seattle)** – KOMO 4 provides up-to-the-minute local news, weather, and live video. It covers Seattle and surrounding areas with frequent updates and trending stories. (Website: komonews.com)
- **KIRO 7 News (CBS Seattle)** – Live, local, in-depth TV news covering Seattle, Tacoma, and Western Washington. KIRO 7 is known for investigative reporting and breaking news that “delivers live, local in-depth” coverage benefiting the community. (Website: kiro7.com)
- **FOX 13 Seattle (KCPQ)** – Seattle’s Fox affiliate, which offers breaking local news, weather, and sports (including Seahawks coverage). FOX 13’s site and broadcasts provide frequent Seattle-area news updates. (Website: fox13seattle.com)
- **KUOW (94.9 FM)** – Seattle’s NPR member station, providing original, independent reporting on local issues. KUOW’s award-winning newsroom produces radio stories, podcasts, and digital articles on Seattle news, politics, and culture. (Website: kuow.org)
- **MyNorthwest** – A news website by Bonneville (KIRO Radio and KTTH) aggregating local news, sports, traffic, and talk. MyNorthwest features stories from KIRO Newsradio, Seattle sports stations, and community contributors. (Website: mynorthwest.com)
- **KNKX (88.5 FM)** – A public radio station (based in Tacoma/Seattle) with regional news and jazz programming. KNKX’s newsroom covers Puget Sound area news and often partners with other public media for deeper stories. (Website: knkx.org)

## Technology and Business News

- **GeekWire** – Seattle’s premier technology news site, with strong roots in the region’s tech industry. GeekWire provides breaking tech news, startup coverage, and analysis of Seattle’s tech business scene. (Website: geekwire.com)
- **Puget Sound Business Journal (PSBJ)** – A weekly business newspaper with daily online updates. PSBJ covers Seattle-area business news across industries – from tech and real estate to healthcare – with frequent headlines throughout the day. (Website: bizjournals.com/seattle)
- **Seattle 24×7** – An online business community publication covering Seattle’s tech and startup ecosystem. Seattle24x7 features stories on local people, companies, and issues in the Pacific Northwest’s internet and business community. (Website: seattle24x7.com)
- **425 Business & 425 Magazine** – Lifestyle and business magazines focused on Seattle’s Eastside (Bellevue, Redmond, etc.). *425 Business* covers the local economy and companies in the greater Puget Sound region. (Websites: 425business.com, 425magazine.com)
- **Seattle Business Magazine** – A monthly magazine (now primarily online) featuring in-depth stories on Seattle-area industries, CEOs, and economic trends. It provides context on the region’s business climate (Website: seattlebusinessmag.com).

## Community and Independent Media

- **South Seattle Emerald** – A nonprofit, community-driven news site serving Seattle’s South End neighborhoods. The Emerald provides in-depth local coverage, amplifying diverse voices and “authentically depicting the dynamic voices, culture, arts, and ideas” of South Seattle. (Website: southseattleemerald.org)
- **The Seattle Medium** – The largest African American-owned newspaper in the Pacific Northwest. The Seattle Medium focuses on local and national news affecting Seattle’s Black community and has been operating for over 50 years. (Website: seattlemedium.com)
- **International Examiner** – A nonprofit Asian American community newspaper based in Seattle’s CID (Chinatown/ID). The Examiner offers news and stories impacting Asian American/Pacific Islander communities in the Seattle area (in print biweekly and online). (Website: iexaminer.org)
- **Northwest Asian Weekly** – A weekly paper providing news for Seattle’s Chinese and Asian communities. It publishes articles on local community news, culture, and immigrant experiences, alongside the biweekly Seattle Chinese Post. (Website: nwasianweekly.com)
- **Seattle Gay News (SGN)** – One of the oldest LGBTQ newspapers in the U.S., published weekly in Seattle. SGN covers LGBTQIA+ news, arts, and issues in Seattle and the Pacific Northwest. (Website: sgn.org)
- **Converge Media** – A leading Black-led Seattle media outlet producing culturally relevant news and stories. Converge Media provides daily urban news briefings, video shows, and community reporting that highlight Seattle’s cultural movements. (Website: whereweconverge.com)
- **PubliCola** – An independent online news site focused on Seattle politics, policy, and local government. Run by veteran local journalists, PubliCola offers scoops and analysis on City Hall, policing, housing, and elections (Website: publicola.com – often updated with insider news).
- **Post Alley** – A Seattle news and commentary blog written by a community of local journalists and civic leaders. Post Alley provides analysis and opinion on Seattle affairs, from politics and transportation to arts and history. (Website: postalley.org)
- **Seattle City Council Insight** – A blog by Kevin Schofield offering in-depth coverage of Seattle City Council proceedings and city legislation. It’s “wordy and nerdy” (in the author’s words) but a go-to for city government news and Council updates (Website: sccinsight.com).
- **Seattle Police Department Blotter (SPD Blotter)** – The **official** Seattle Police Department news blog posting crime blotter updates, significant incident reports, and police news releases. SPD Blotter is updated frequently with information on crimes, arrests, and public safety alerts straight from SPD. (Website: spdblotter.seattle.gov)

## Seattle Neighborhood & Hyperlocal Blogs

- **Capitol Hill Seattle Blog (CHS)** – A long-running community news site for Capitol Hill and Central Seattle. CHS covers neighborhood news, crime, real estate, events, and everyday life on “the Hill,” incorporating voices from around the community. (Website: capitolhillseattle.com)
- **West Seattle Blog** – A 24/7 news source for the West Seattle peninsula. The West Seattle Blog provides round-the-clock neighborhood news, including breaking stories, traffic alerts, development updates, and community happenings. (Website: westseattleblog.com)
- **My Ballard** – A neighborhood blog covering Seattle’s Ballard (and nearby Fremont) area. My Ballard reports on local businesses, restaurants, events, and community news for Ballard residents. (Website: myballard.com)
- **B-Town Blog (Burien)** – A popular independent blog focused on Burien, just south of Seattle. It delivers hyperlocal Burien news – city council updates, community events, crime incidents – often scooping bigger outlets. (Website: btownblog.com)
- **UW News/The Daily** – The Daily of the University of Washington is a student-run newspaper published on weekdays during the school year. While campus-focused, it sometimes covers city issues and events in the U-District. (Website: thedaily.washington.edu)
- **Seattle Reddit Community (r/Seattle)** – While not a traditional outlet, the Seattle subreddit is a crowdsourced hub for local news and discussion. Over 400k members share breaking news links, community questions, and real-time updates (from transit delays to local memes). It’s a useful daily aggregator of what Seattleites are talking about.
- **Nextdoor Neighborhood Feeds** – Seattle’s neighborhoods are very active on Nextdoor, where residents post community alerts (like lost pets, safety concerns, or local recommendations). Though informal, Nextdoor can surface ultra-hyperlocal news (e.g. a burst water main on your block) before any media.

*(Many Seattle neighborhood news sites exist beyond these, including community association blogs and Facebook groups for areas like **Queen Anne & Magnolia News**, **Beacon Hill**, **Rainier Valley**, etc. The above list highlights a few of the most active and widely read hyperlocal sources.)*

## Greater Seattle Area Local News (Metro & Suburbs)

For news in cities around Seattle (King County and nearby), several community newspapers and blogs keep locals informed:

- **Bellevue Reporter** – Local newspaper covering Bellevue news, Eastside business, schools, and city hall developments. (Part of Sound Publishing’s community newspaper network). (Website: bellevuereporter.com)
- **Redmond Reporter** – News site focused on Redmond, including city government news, Microsoft/tech community updates, and local events. (Website: redmond-reporter.com)
- **Kirkland Reporter** – Community news source for Kirkland, reporting on development, public safety, and events on the north Eastside. (Website: kirklandreporter.com)
- **Issaquah Reporter** – Covers Issaquah and the Snoqualmie Valley, with stories on local schools, mountain community events, and suburban growth. (Website: issaquahreporter.com)
- **Mercer Island Reporter** – News from Mercer Island, including city council happenings, local issues and community features. (Website: mi-reporter.com)
- **Bothell/Kenmore Reporter** – Serves North King County (Bothell, Kenmore) with neighborhood news, city developments, and community sports. (Website: bothell-reporter.com)
- **Renton Reporter** – Local news in Renton, from downtown developments and Boeing news to high school sports and crime. (Website: rentonreporter.com)
- **Kent Reporter** – Covers Kent and Covington area news (South King County), including local government, businesses, and community events. (Website: kentreporter.com)
- **Covington-Maple Valley Reporter** – Focused on the Covington and Maple Valley communities. It reports on local city news, Maple Valley’s rapid growth, and issues in southeast King County. (Website: covingtonreporter.com)
- **Auburn Reporter** – News for the city of Auburn (south of Seattle), covering local council news, the Muckleshoot community, and local happenings. (Website: auburn-reporter.com)
- **Federal Way Mirror** – A weekly paper and site for Federal Way, with city news, politics, and community stories (e.g., happenings at the Commons Mall, school district news). (Website: federalwaymirror.com)
- **Everett Herald** – Snohomish County’s daily paper based in Everett. The Herald reports on regional news just north of Seattle (Everett, Lynnwood, etc.), including business (Boeing), county government, and North Sound events. (Website: heraldnet.com)

*(All the above “Reporter” newspapers are part of Sound Publishing, which operates dozens of community newspapers around Puget Sound. They are free to read online and updated regularly, though many are weeklies.)*

## Food, Culture, and Lifestyle Sources

- **Seattle Met (Seattle Metropolitan)** – A glossy city magazine publishing engaging features on Seattle culture, dining, arts, and neighborhoods. Seattle Met (published monthly by SagaCity Media) is known as “the city’s indispensable news, culture, and lifestyle magazine”. (Website: seattlemet.com)
- **Seattle Magazine** – Another long-running monthly magazine that highlights the best of Seattle’s dining, arts, events, and urban life. Seattle Magazine’s coverage ranges from restaurant guides to local travel and profiles, showcasing Seattle culture. (Website: seattlemag.com)
- **Eater Seattle** – A dedicated Seattle food and dining news site (part of the Eater network). It obsessively covers restaurant openings/closings, chef news, bar and cafe guides, and Seattle food trends. If you want daily updates on where to eat in Seattle, Eater is invaluable. (Website: seattle.eater.com)
- **CHS Capitol Hill Seattle** – *[Listed above under Neighborhood Blogs]* – Worth noting again for culture: CHS also covers Capitol Hill’s food and nightlife scene, from new bars to arts events, in addition to news.
- **The Stranger’s Slog & Events** – *[Listed under Major Publications]* – The Stranger’s daily blog (“Slog”) provides snarky coverage of arts, music, and city happenings. The Stranger is also a key source for events, with comprehensive calendars for concerts, movies, theater, and nightlife. (Website: thestranger.com)
- **Visit Seattle (Official Tourism Site)** – The official visitor bureau site offers event calendars and guides to Seattle attractions. While promotional in nature, it features blogs on local culture, lists of things to do, and seasonal guides (“25 Things To Do in Seattle in 2025,” etc.). Good for discovering festivals, arts events, and new attractions. (Website: visitseattle.org)
- **Seattle Arts & Music Blogs** – Several niche blogs serve Seattle’s arts scene. For example, *Seattle Theater Group’s blog* highlights performances, and *KEXP’s blog* covers the local music scene. **Seattle Stage** and **City Arts** (magazine) also provide arts coverage. These can be explored for specific interests (e.g., live music, theater, visual arts).

## Social Media and News Aggregators

- **Twitter (X)** – Most Seattle news outlets and many reporters are active on X (formerly Twitter). Following accounts like **@seattletimes**, **@komonews**, **@SeattlePD** (Seattle Police Department), or journalists like Erica C. Barnett and Mike Carter can yield real-time news updates. Twitter is often where breaking news (snow closures, protests, quake reports) appears first from official sources. *(No account needed to view tweets; search for Seattle topics or hashtags like **#Seattle** for trending news.)*
- **Mastodon & Bluesky** – A number of Seattle journalists and tech workers have migrated to decentralized platforms. For instance, you can find Seattle Times reporters on Mastodon (often on mastodon.social or journa.host instances) and local tech figures on Bluesky. While these networks are smaller, they can be useful for niche discussions – e.g. following @SeaTransportation on Mastodon for transit chatter. (Check if your favorite news people have shared their Mastodon or Bluesky handles.)
- **Reddit (r/Seattle and r/SeattleWA)** – As mentioned, Reddit’s Seattle communities aggregate news and host discussion. **r/Seattle** is the largest, and **r/SeattleWA** is a smaller moderated alternative. Reddit users frequently post news articles (with direct links to sources above) and first-person updates (like eyewitness accounts of events or traffic jams). It’s a good daily digest of what’s happening and how locals feel about it.
- **Google News & Alerts** – For a broad daily sweep of Seattle news, Google News has a “Seattle” section that pulls top stories from many of the above sources. You can customize Google News or set up an email alert for “Seattle” to get a feed of headlines (from major incidents to fun features) aggregated in one place. This can complement the specific sources by ensuring you don’t miss big stories outside your usual reading.
- **Smart News Aggregators** – Apps and sites like *Seattle Brief* (an AI-driven news aggregator) collect Seattle-related news from multiple outlets and summarize them. Similarly, Apple News or Flipboard can be configured to show local Seattle news by following topics or specific outlets. These tools are useful if you prefer a single feed interface – just be sure to include the high-quality sources listed above in your feed.
